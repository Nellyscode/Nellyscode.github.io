Happy Stack

A social emotional learning picture book tailored for children ages 4 to 8 years old, that shows how memories can uplift spirits even during challenging times. Throughout their daily rituals, a young girl discovers subtle clues that her grandmother is not quite the same. Happy Stack is about an experience with Alzheimers, but it focuses more on the joy and connection between a granddaughter and her grandmother. Through the actions of the young protagonist, readers will learn about empathy, helping others, and cherishing memories, even in the face of difficult circumstances.

Yummitate

A picture book tailored for children ages 4 to 8. This story explores the power of mindful eating. It empowers children to listen to their bodies and enjoy their food. Through this interactive story, they'll discover how eating mindfully can help prevent bellyaches, make meals more satisfying, and discover how using all five senses can make each meal an adventure. YUMMITATE encourages slowing down, paying attention, and enjoying each bite with gratitude.

Nana's Remedy

A social-emotinal learning picture book tailored for children ages 4 to 8. A young girl is determined to win Sunnyside's competition. Nana cheers her on:Corre! Balance! Brinca! Crawl!She goes faster and faster until—OUCH! She gets hurt. To comfort her, Nana shares a magical rhyme passed down through generations. The rhyme bringshealing and laughter, but Ella is still scared to try again. Now, she must discover the true magic behind Nana's chant to overcome her fears and take on the challenge once again.

Pink Lemonade

A story about grit, entrepreneurship, and how big dreams can grow. When a determined young girl falls in love with a toy airplane but her mom can't afford it, she does not give up. Instead, she uses her grandmother's special lemonade recipe to earn the money herself.Each time her dream grows, from buying the toy plane, to taking a flight, to learning to fly, and finally becoming a pilot, she finds a way to fund it. Years later, now a pilot, she meets another little girl selling lemonade with an even bigger dream than her own.
